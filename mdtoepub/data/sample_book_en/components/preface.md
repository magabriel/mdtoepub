This novel was born of a chance discovery. In the summer of 2019, while researching at the National Archives in London for a paper on nineteenth-century British diplomatic correspondence, I came across a letter dated 1897 that had been incorrectly catalogued and lay forgotten in the wrong file.

The letter, written by a certain E. W. Ashford, mentioned a "vestige of centuries" that had been discovered during restoration work in a church in the West Country. The tone of the writing, caught between excitement and fear, intrigued me so deeply that I decided to follow its trail.

What began as a simple academic curiosity became an investigation that led me through archives, libraries, and historic buildings across half a dozen English cities. And although this novel is a work of fiction, many of the letters, documents, and places that appear in it are real, or are based on real elements that I uncovered along the way.

I ask only one thing of the reader: let yourself be carried away. Accept the invitation to traverse these pages as one traverses an old building, knowing that behind every closed door a discovery may await, and that the dust covering old things sometimes protects unexpected treasures.

Elena M. Vasquez
London, spring of 2025
