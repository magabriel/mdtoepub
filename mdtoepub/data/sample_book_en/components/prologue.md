# Prologue

On the night of 14 November 1888, Dr. Edmund Hargrove left the British Museum with a book under his arm and was never seen alive again.

Three days later, his body was found in the Thames, near the Tower of London. In his pocket was a handwritten note bearing a single word: *Sigilum*.

The book was never found.

The case was closed for lack of evidence, and Hargrove's name was buried in the city's police archives. But there were those who, years later, would remember that story.

And there were those who, upon stumbling upon it, could not let it go.
