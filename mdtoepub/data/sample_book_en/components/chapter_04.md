*Bath, 15 July 1898*

The Secret Library of Bath appeared on no map, in no catalogue, and in no travel guide. It existed only in the memory of a few scholars who mentioned it in hushed tones, as one shares a secret.

Ashford had found his way to it thanks to a letter of introduction from Professor Raymond Cross, who, after several meetings in Edinburgh, had agreed to assist him. The professor was an elderly man with a white beard and a penetrating gaze, who kept in his private study a collection of facsimiles of the documents found at St. Benedict's.

—The original codex is not here —Cross had told him, showing the facsimiles—. But these are sufficient to know that we have something extraordinary in our hands. Look.

The characters of the codex bore no resemblance to anything Ashford had seen before. They vaguely recalled Gothic script, but with a strange twist, as though each letter had been written before a mirror.

—It is cryptography —said Cross—. But not conventional cryptography. The author of this codex did not simply wish to conceal his message: he wished that no one should even know there was a message to conceal.

—And you believe the Bath Library may hold the key.

—It is said —Cross lowered his voice— that among its holdings there is a cryptography treatise from the sixteenth century. A manual. If anyone can decipher this, it is that book.

The Secret Library was hidden beneath the ground floor of a nineteenth-century manor house on the slopes of the Bath hills. Access was through a trapdoor concealed under a Persian rug in the main library of the house, which in turn served as the private residence of the last descendant of a family of bibliophiles.

Ashford was received by the librarian himself, a silent, bony man who guided him downstairs without uttering a single word. The underground chamber was larger than Ashford had imagined, illuminated by gas lamps whose flames flickered in the cut-glass fixtures.

In the centre of the room, upon a lectern of dark wood, rested a book of medium size, bound in embossed leather with gilt tooling.

It was the cryptography manual.

Ashford held his breath and reached out his hand.
