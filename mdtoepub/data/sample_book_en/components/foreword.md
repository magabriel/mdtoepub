When Elena M. Vasquez asked me to write a foreword for this novel, I had no idea how deeply *The Vestige of Centuries* would captivate me from its very first pages. The book the reader now holds is not merely a historical thriller: it is a journey through the strata of time, an inquiry into how the past refuses to remain silent.

Vasquez, known for her previous non-fiction historical works, demonstrates here a remarkable command of fiction. Her prose, precise and evocative, builds an atmosphere that envelops the reader from the first paragraph and does not release them until the final full stop.

The historical research underpinning the plot is impeccable. The settings of London, Oxford, and Edinburgh are portrayed with a detail that only someone who has walked their streets and breathed their history could achieve.

I recommend this work to those seeking a reading that combines the pleasure of a good story with the richness of the late nineteenth-century British historical context.

Dr. Richard H. Thornton
Professor of Modern History
University of Oxford
