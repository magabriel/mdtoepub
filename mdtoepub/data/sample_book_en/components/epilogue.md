# Epilogue

*Bath, summer of 1901*

The house was small, built of granite with a slate roof, perched on one of the hills that surround the Royal Crescent. From the attic window one could see the Georgian terraces and, beyond, the hazy blue of the Bristol Channel.

Ashford had needed two years to arrange all the material. The documents from the warehouse, Hargrove's letters, the star maps, the interviews with the last witnesses. Little by little, the puzzle had taken shape.

He would not publish the results. Not at least during the lifetime of those involved.

But he had left a leather-bound copy in the library of the University of Oxford, with instructions that it not be opened until 1960.

Once more, time would have the last word.
