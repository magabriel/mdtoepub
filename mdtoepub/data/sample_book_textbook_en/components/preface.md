This book grew out of a simple observation: most people are never taught *how* to learn.

We spend years in school being told *what* to learn — mathematics, history, science, languages — but the question of *how* to learn effectively is rarely addressed. The result is that millions of students and professionals rely on strategies that feel intuitive but are, in fact, among the least effective: re-reading, highlighting, and cramming.

The science of learning has advanced enormously in the past three decades. We now know, with a high degree of confidence, which strategies work and which do not. We understand the mechanisms of memory, the role of attention, the power of retrieval practice, and the importance of mindset. Yet this knowledge remains largely confined to academic journals, inaccessible to the very people who could benefit from it most.

This book is my attempt to bridge that gap.

Every chapter is grounded in peer-reviewed research, but the emphasis is always on practical application. You will find exercises, examples, and actionable strategies that you can begin using immediately — whether you are a student preparing for examinations, a professional acquiring new skills, or simply someone who wants to understand how your own mind works.

I do not promise miracles. Learning is effortful, and there are no shortcuts. But I do promise that if you apply the principles in this book, you will learn more effectively, more deeply, and more durably than you ever have before.

Clara M. Rivers
Cambridge, autumn of 2024
